      <h2>coercion</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : use of force to get someone to object; compelling; V. coerce</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the act of compelling by force of authority
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) using force to cause something to occur

      <h2>cognate</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : having a common origin; related linguistically; allied by blood; similar or akin in nature; Ex. cognate languages; N.</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) one related by blood or origin; especially on sharing an ancestor with another
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/blood relation">blood relation</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/blood relative">blood relative</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/sib">sib</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a word is cognate with another if both derive from the same word in an ancestral language
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/cognate word">cognate word</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) related in nature
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/connate">connate</a>

            
             
         </div>

         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) having the same ancestral language
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  cognate languages               </li>
