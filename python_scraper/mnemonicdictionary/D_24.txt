      <h2>deviate</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : turn away from (a principle, norm); move away from an accepted standard; swerve; depart; diverge; N. deviation; Ex. deviation of the path of light by a prism</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a person whose behavior deviates from what is acceptable especially in sexual behavior
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/degenerate">degenerate</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/deviant">deviant</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/pervert">pervert</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) turn aside; turn away from
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/divert">divert</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) be at variance with; be out of line with
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/depart">depart</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/diverge">diverge</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/vary">vary</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) cause to turn away from a previous or expected course
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  The river was deviated to prevent flooding               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) markedly different from an accepted norm
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/aberrant">aberrant</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/deviant">deviant</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  aberrant behavior               </li>
                              <li>
                  deviant ideas               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>

      <h2>devious</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : roundabout; erratic; deviating from the straight course; not straightforward; not completely honest; Ex. devious route</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) indirect in departing from the accepted or proper way; misleading
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/oblique">oblique</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  used devious means to achieve success               </li>
                              <li>
                  gave oblique answers to direct questions               </li>
                              <li>
                  oblique political maneuvers               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) characterized by insincerity or deceit; evasive
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/shifty">shifty</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  a devious character               </li>
                              <li>
                  shifty eyes               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) deviating from a straight course
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/circuitous">circuitous</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/roundabout">roundabout</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  a scenic but devious route               </li>
                              <li>
                  a long and circuitous journey by train and boat               </li>
                              <li>
