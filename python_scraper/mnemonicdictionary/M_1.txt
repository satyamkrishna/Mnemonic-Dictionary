      <h2>mace</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : ceremonial staff used as a symbol of authority; clublike medieval weapon</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) (trademark) a liquid that temporarily disables a person; prepared as an aerosol and sprayed in the face, it irritates the eyes and causes dizziness and immobilization
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/chemical mace">chemical mace</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) an official who carries a mace of office
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/macebearer">macebearer</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/macer">macer</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) spice made from the dried fleshy covering of the nutmeg seed
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a ceremonial staff carried as a symbol of office or authority
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

               </div>
