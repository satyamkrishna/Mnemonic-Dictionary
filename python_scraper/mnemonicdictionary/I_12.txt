      <h2>implicate</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : incriminate; involve incriminatingly; show to be involved (in a crime); Ex. implicate someone in the crime</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) bring into intimate and incriminating connection
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  He is implicated in the scheme to defraud the government               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) impose, involve, or imply as a necessary accompaniment or result

      <h2>implication</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : something hinted at or suggested; implying; implicating</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) something that is inferred (deduced or entailed or implied)
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/deduction">deduction</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/entailment">entailment</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  his resignation had political implications               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a meaning that is not expressly stated but can be inferred
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/import">import</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/significance">significance</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  the significance of his remark became clear only later               </li>
                              <li>
                  the expectation was spread both by word and by implication               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) an accusation that brings into intimate and usually incriminating connection
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a logical relation between propositions p and q of the form `if p then q'; if p is true then q cannot be false
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/conditional relation">conditional relation</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/logical implication">logical implication</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a relation implicated by virtue of involvement or close connection (especially an incriminating involvement)
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  he was suspected of implication in several robberies               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

               </div>

      <h2>implicit</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : understood but not stated; implied; unquestioning and complete; Ex. implicit trust</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) implied though not directly expressed; inherent in the nature of something
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/inexplicit">inexplicit</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  an implicit agreement not to raise the subject               </li>
                              <li>
                  there was implicit criticism in his voice               </li>
                              <li>
                  anger was implicit in the argument               </li>
                              <li>
                  the oak is implicit in the acorn               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) being without doubt or reserve
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/unquestioning">unquestioning</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  implicit trust               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

               </div>

                  <div class="row-fluid">
