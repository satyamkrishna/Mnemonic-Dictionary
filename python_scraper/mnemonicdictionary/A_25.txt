      <h2>aloft</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : upward; high up in the air</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adv) at or on or to the masthead or upper rigging of a ship
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  climbed aloft to unfurl the sail               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adv) upward
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  the good news sent her spirits aloft               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adv) at or to great height; high up in or into the air
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  eagles were soaring aloft               </li>
                              <li>
                  dust is whirled aloft               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adv) in the higher atmosphere above the earth
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>

      <h2>aloof</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : apart; not open in one's relationship with other people; reserved; ADV.</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) remote in manner
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/distant">distant</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/upstage">upstage</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  stood apart with aloof dignity               </li>
                              <li>
                  a distant smile               </li>
                              <li>
                  he was upstage with strangers               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adv) in an aloof manner
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  the local gentry and professional classes had held aloof for the school had accepted their sons readily enough               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

               </div>
