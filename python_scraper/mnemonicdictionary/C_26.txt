      <h2>circuitous</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : roundabout</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) marked by obliqueness or indirection in speech or conduct
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/roundabout">roundabout</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  the explanation was circuitous and puzzling               </li>
                              <li>
                  a roundabout paragraph               </li>
                              <li>
                  hear in a roundabout way that her ex-husband was marrying her best friend               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) deviating from a straight course
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/devious">devious</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/roundabout">roundabout</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  a scenic but devious route               </li>
                              <li>
                  a long and circuitous journey by train and boat               </li>
                              <li>
                  a roundabout route avoided rush-hour traffic               </li>
                           </ul>

      <h2>circumscribe</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : limit; confine; draw a line around</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) draw a line around
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  He drew a circle around the points               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) restrict or confine,
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/confine">confine</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/limit">limit</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  I limit you to two visits to the pub a day               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) to draw a geometric figure around another figure so that the two are in contact but do not intersect
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

               </div>
