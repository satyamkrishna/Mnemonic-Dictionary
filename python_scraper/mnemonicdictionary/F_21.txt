      <h2>flux</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : flowing; series of changes; fluctuation; Ex. in a state of flux</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the rate of flow of energy or particles across a given surface
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a flow or discharge
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/fluxion">fluxion</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a substance added to molten metals to bond with impurities that can then be readily removed
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) excessive discharge of liquid from a cavity or organ (as in watery diarrhea)
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a state of uncertainty about what should be done (usually following some important event) preceding the establishment of a new direction of action
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/state of flux">state of flux</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  the flux following the death of the emperor               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the lines of force surrounding a permanent magnet or a moving charged particle
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/magnetic field">magnetic field</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/magnetic flux">magnetic flux</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) (physics) the number of changes in energy flow across a given surface per unit area
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/flux density">flux density</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) in constant change
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  his opinions are in flux               </li>
                              <li>
                  the newness and flux of the computer industry               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) move or progress freely as if in a stream
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/flow">flow</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />

      <h2>fodder</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : coarse food for cattle, horses, etc.; feed for livestock; CF. food</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) soldiers who are regarded as expendable in the face of artillery fire
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/cannon fodder">cannon fodder</a>

                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/fresh fish">fresh fish</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) coarse food (especially for livestock) composed of entire plants or the leaves and stalks of a cereal crop
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) give fodder (to domesticated animals)
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>
