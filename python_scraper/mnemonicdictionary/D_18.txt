      <h2>deposition</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : testimony under oath; deposing; dethroning</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the natural process of laying down a deposit of something
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/deposit">deposit</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) (law) a pretrial interrogation of a witness; usually conducted in a lawyer's office
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the act of putting something somewhere
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/deposit">deposit</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the act of deposing someone; removing a powerful person from a position or office
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">

      <h2>depravity</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : extreme corruption; wickedness; V. deprave</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) moral perversion; impairment of virtue and moral principles
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/corruption">corruption</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/degeneracy">degeneracy</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/depravation">depravation</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/putrefaction">putrefaction</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  the luxury and corruption among the upper classes               </li>
                              <li>
                  moral degeneracy followed intellectual degeneration               </li>
                              <li>
                  its brothels, its opium parlors, its depravity               </li>
                              <li>
                  Rome had fallen into moral putrefaction               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a corrupt or depraved or degenerate act or practice
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/turpitude">turpitude</a>

            
             
         </div>
