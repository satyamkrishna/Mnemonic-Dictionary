      <h2>equity</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : fairness; justice; OP. inequity</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the difference between the market value of a property and the claims held against it
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the ownership interest of shareholders in a corporation
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) conformity with rules or standards

                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/fairness">fairness</a>

      <h2>equivocal</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : (of words or statements) ambiguous; intentionally misleading; (of behavior) questionable; OP. unequivocal</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) open to two or more interpretations; or of uncertain nature or significance; or (often) intended to mislead
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/ambiguous">ambiguous</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  an equivocal statement               </li>
                              <li>
                  the polling had a complex and equivocal (or ambiguous) message for potential female candidates               </li>
                              <li>
                  the officer's equivocal behavior increased the victim's uneasiness               </li>
                              <li>
                  popularity is an equivocal crown               </li>
                              <li>
                  an equivocal response to an embarrassing question               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) open to question
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  aliens of equivocal loyalty               </li>
                              <li>
                  his conscience reproached him with the equivocal character of the union into which he had forced his son               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) uncertain as a sign or indication
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  the evidence from bacteriologic analysis was equivocal               </li>
                           </ul>
