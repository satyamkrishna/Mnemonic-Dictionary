      <h2>meander</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : wind or turn in its course; follow a winding or turning course; move aimlessly and idly</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a bend or curve, as in a stream or river
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) an aimless amble on a winding course
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/ramble">ramble</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) to move or cause to move in a sinuous, spiral, or circular course
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/thread">thread</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/wander">wander</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/weave">weave</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/wind">wind</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />

                              <li>
                  the river winds through the hills               </li>

                  the path meanders through the vineyards               </li>

      <h2>mediate</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : settle a dispute through the services of an outsider; act as an intermediary; produce by mediating; Ex. mediate a cease-fire</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) act between parties with a view to reconciling differences
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/arbitrate">arbitrate</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/intercede">intercede</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/intermediate">intermediate</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/liaise">liaise</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  He interceded in the family dispute               </li>
                              <li>
                  He mediated a settlement               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) occupy an intermediate or middle position or form a connecting link or stage between two others
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  mediate between the old and the new               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) acting through or dependent on an intervening agency
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  the disease spread by mediate as well as direct contact               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) being neither at the beginning nor at the end in a series
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/in-between">in-between</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/middle">middle</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  adolescence is an awkward in-between age               </li>
                              <li>
                  in a mediate position               </li>
                              <li>
                  the middle point on a line               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>

      <h2>mediocre</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : ordinary; commonplace; neither good nor bad</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) moderate to inferior in quality
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/second-rate">second-rate</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  they improved the quality from mediocre to above average               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) lacking exceptional quality or ability
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/average">average</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/fair">fair</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/middling">middling</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />

                  a novel of average merit               </li>
                              <li>
                  only a fair performance of the sonata               </li>
                              <li>
                  in fair health               </li>
                              <li>
                  the caliber of the students has gone from mediocre to above average               </li>

                  the performance was middling at best               </li>

         <u>Definition</u><br />
