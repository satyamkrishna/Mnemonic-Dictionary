      <h2>contagion</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : infection (by contact); ADJ. contagious; CF. infectious: that can be passed by infection in the air</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) any disease easily transmitted by contact
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/contagious disease">contagious disease</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) an incident in which an infectious disease is transmitted
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/infection">infection</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/transmission">transmission</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the communication of an attitude or emotional state among a number of people
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/infection">infection</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  a contagion of mirth               </li>
                              <li>
                  the infection of his enthusiasm for poetry               </li>
                           </ul>
