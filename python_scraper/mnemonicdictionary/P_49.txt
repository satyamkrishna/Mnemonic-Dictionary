      <h2>premise</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : assumption; postulate; proposition upon which an argument is based</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a statement that is assumed to be true and from which a conclusion can be drawn
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/assumption">assumption</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/premiss">premiss</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  on the assumption that he has been injured we can infer that he will not to play               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) set forth beforehand, often as an explanation
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  He premised these remarks so that his readers might understand               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) furnish with a preface or introduction
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/introduce">introduce</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/precede">precede</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/preface">preface</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  She always precedes her lectures with a joke               </li>
                              <li>
                  He prefaced his lecture with a critical remark about the institution               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) take something as preexisting and given
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/premiss">premiss</a>

      <h2>premonition</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : forewarning; presentiment; foreboding</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a feeling of evil to come
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/boding">boding</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/foreboding">foreboding</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/presentiment">presentiment</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  a steadily escalating sense of foreboding               </li>
                              <li>
                  the lawyer had a presentiment that the judge would dismiss the case               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) an early warning about a future event
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/forewarning">forewarning</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

               </div>
