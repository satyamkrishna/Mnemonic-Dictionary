      <h2>vindicate</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : clear from blame; free from blame or accusation (with supporting proof); exonerate; substantiate; justify or support; avenge; Ex. vindicate one's client; Ex. vindicate one's claim; CF. vindicator</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) show to be right by providing justification or proof
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/justify">justify</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  vindicate a claim               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) maintain, uphold, or defend
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  vindicate the rights of the citizens               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) clear of accusation, blame, suspicion, or doubt with supporting proof
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  You must vindicate yourself and fight this libel               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

      <h2>vindictive</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : disposed to revenge; vengeful; out for revenge; spiteful; intended to cause harm; malicious; Ex. vindictive streak</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) disposed to seek revenge or intended for revenge
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/revengeful">revengeful</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/vengeful">vengeful</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>

                  more vindictive than jealous love               </li>

                  punishments...essentially vindictive in their nature               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) showing malicious ill will and a desire to hurt; motivated by spite
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/despiteful">despiteful</a>
