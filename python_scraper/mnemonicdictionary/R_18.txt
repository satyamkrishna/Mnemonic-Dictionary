      <h2>regeneration</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : spiritual rebirth; Ex. regeneration of the prisoners; V. regenerate: give or obtain new life; reform spiritually</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) (biology) growth anew of lost tissue or destroyed parts or organs
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) feedback in phase with (augmenting) the input
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/positive feedback">positive feedback</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the activity of spiritual or physical renewal
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) forming again (especially with improvements or removal of defects); renewing and reconstituting
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/re-formation">re-formation</a>

            
             
         </div>

      <h2>regent</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : a person who governs in place of a ruler who is ill, absent, or still a child; ADJ. Ex. the Prince regent</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) members of a governing board
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/trustee">trustee</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) someone who rules during the absence or incapacity or minority of the country's monarch
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) acting or functioning as a regent or ruler
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>

      <h2>regime</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : method of system or government</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the organization that is the governing authority of a political unit
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/authorities">authorities</a>

         </div>

                           </ul>

         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) (medicine) a systematic plan for therapy (often including diet)

            <u>Synonyms</u> :
