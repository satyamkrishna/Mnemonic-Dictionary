      <h2>indemnify</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : make secure against damage or loss; compensate for damage or loss; CF. make uninjured</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) secure against future loss, damage, or liability; give security for
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  This plan indemnifies workers against wages lost through illness               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) make amends for; pay compensation for

            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/compensate">compensate</a>

                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/recompense">recompense</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/repair">repair</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  One can never fully repair the suffering and losses of the Jews in the Third Reich               </li>

      <h2>indentation</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : notch; deep recess; V. indent; CF. tooth</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a concave cut into a surface or edge (as in a coastline)
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/indenture">indenture</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the formation of small pits in a surface as a consequence of corrosion
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/pitting">pitting</a>

         </div>

         <u>Definition</u><br />

                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :

      <h2>indenture</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : bind as servant or apprentice to master; bind by indenture; N: contract binding one party into the service of another for a specified time (as between an apprentice and his master)</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a concave cut into a surface or edge (as in a coastline)
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/indentation">indentation</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) formal agreement between the issuer of bonds and the bondholders as to terms of the debt
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a contract binding one party into the service of another for a specified term
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the space left between the margin and the start of an indented line
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/indent">indent</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/indentation">indentation</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/indention">indention</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) bind by or as if by indentures, as of an apprentice or servant
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/indent">indent</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />

                  an indentured servant               </li>

      <h2>indeterminate</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : uncertain; not clearly fixed; indefinite</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) not precisely determined or established; not fixed or known in advance
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/undetermined">undetermined</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />

                              <li>
                  an indeterminate point of law               </li>
                              <li>
                  the influence of environment is indeterminate               </li>
                              <li>
                  an indeterminate future               </li>
                           </ul>

         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) of uncertain or ambiguous nature
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  the equivocal (or indeterminate) objects painted by surrealists               </li>

         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) not capable of being determined
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  the indeterminate number of plant species in the jungle               </li>

         <u>Definition</u><br />
