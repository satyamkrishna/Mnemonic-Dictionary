      <h2>affirmation</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : positive assertion; confirmation; solemn pledge by one who refuses to take an oath; V. affirm; ADJ. affirmative; CF. affirmative action: positive discrimination</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a statement asserting the existence or the truth of something
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/avouchment">avouchment</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/avowal">avowal</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the act of affirming or asserting or stating something
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/assertion">assertion</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/statement">statement</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) (religion) a solemn declaration that serves the same purpose as an oath (if an oath is objectionable to the person on religious or ethical grounds)
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a judgment by a higher court that the judgment of a lower court was correct and should stand

      <h2>affliction</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : state of distress; trial; cause of distress or suffering; V. afflict: inflict grievous suffering on</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a state of great suffering and distress due to adversity
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a condition of suffering or distress due to ill health
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a cause of great suffering and distress

      <h2>agape</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : openmouthed</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) (Christian theology) the love of God or Christ for mankind
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) selfless love of one person for another without sexual implications (especially love that is spiritual in nature)
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :
