      <h2>partition</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : divide into parts</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a vertical structure that divides or separates (as a wall divides one room from another)
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/divider">divider</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) (computer science) the part of a hard disk that is dedicated to a particular operating system or application and accessed as a single unit
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) (anatomy) a structure that separates areas in an organism
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the act of dividing or partitioning; separation by the creation of a boundary that divides or keeps apart
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/division">division</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/partitioning">partitioning</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/sectionalisation">sectionalisation</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/sectionalization">sectionalization</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/segmentation">segmentation</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (verb) divide into parts, pieces, or sections
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/partition off">partition off</a>

            
             
         </div>

                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">

      <h2>passive</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : not active; acted upon; receiving an action without acting in return; accepting without resistance</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the voice used to indicate that the grammatical subject of the verb is the recipient (not the source) of the action denoted by the verb
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/passive voice">passive voice</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  `The ball was thrown by the boy' uses the passive voice               </li>
                              <li>
                  `The ball was thrown' is an abbreviated passive               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) lacking in energy or will
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/inactive">inactive</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>

                  Much benevolence of the passive order may be traced to a disinclination to inflict pain upon oneself               </li>
                           </ul>

         (adj) peacefully resistant in response to injustice
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/peaceful">peaceful</a>

            <ul>
                              <li>
                  passive resistance               </li>
