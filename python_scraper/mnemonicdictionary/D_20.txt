      <h2>derivation</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : deriving; origin or source of something; Ex. the derivation of the word</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the source or origin from which something derives (i.e. comes or issues)
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  he prefers shoes of Italian derivation               </li>
                              <li>
                  music of Turkish derivation               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) (historical linguistics) an explanation of the historical origins of a word or phrase
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/deriving">deriving</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/etymologizing">etymologizing</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a line of reasoning that shows how a conclusion follows logically from accepted propositions
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) (descriptive linguistics) the process whereby new words are formed from existing words or bases by affixation
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  `singer' from `sing' or `undo' from `do' are examples of derivations               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) inherited properties shared with others of your bloodline
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/ancestry">ancestry</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/filiation">filiation</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/lineage">lineage</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) drawing of fluid or inflammation away from a diseased part of the body
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) drawing off water from its main channel as for irrigation
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the act of deriving something or obtaining something from a source or origin

      <h2>derivative</h2>
      <p><strong>Short Definition</strong> : unoriginal; obtained from another source; Ex. derivative prose style; N.</p>

         
      <div style="padding-bottom:0px;">
         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) the result of mathematical differentiation; the instantaneous change of one quantity relative to another; df(x)/dx
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/derived function">derived function</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/differential">differential</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/differential coefficient">differential coefficient</a>

                        ,
            
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/first derivative">first derivative</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a compound obtained from, or regarded as derived from, another compound
         
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) a financial instrument whose value is based on another security
         
                  <div style="padding-top:8px;">
            <u>Synonyms</u> :           
                        <a href="http://www.mnemonicdictionary.com/word/derivative instrument">derivative instrument</a>

            
             
         </div>
          

            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (noun) (linguistics) a word that is derived from another word
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  `electricity' is a derivative of `electric'               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>

         

         <u>Definition</u><br />      
         (adj) resulting from or employing derivation
         
          

                 
         <div style="padding-top: 10px;"><u>Example Sentence</u> <br />
            <ul>
                              <li>
                  a derivative process               </li>
                              <li>
                  a highly derivative prose style               </li>
                           </ul>
         </div>
            
         <hr style="margin-top:24px;margin-bottom:5px;"/>
